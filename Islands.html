Planning a trip to Palawan and looking for the perfect place to enjoy? Islands

Snake Island

Snake Island, situated in Bacuit Bay in El Nido, Palawan, is renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty. What makes this island unique is the sandbar that emerges during low tide, connecting two distinct landmasses and forming a shape reminiscent of a snake. The sandbar is surrounded by crystal-clear turquoise waters, making it an ideal spot for snorkeling and swimming. Visitors to Snake Island can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding limestone cliffs, lush greenery, and the sparkling waters of Bacuit Bay. The island is part of the Bacuit Archipelago, a group of stunning limestone islands and islets that contribute to the scenic landscape of Palawan.

Entalula Island

Entalula Island is a picturesque and relatively small island known for its pristine white sand beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and vibrant coral reefs. As part of the Bacuit Archipelago in El Nido, Palawan, Entalula Island attracts visitors with its serene ambiance and natural beauty. The island is often included in island-hopping tours, allowing tourists to explore its stunning surroundings and indulge in various water activities. Snorkeling is a popular choice, as the waters around Entalula are home to a diverse array of marine life, including colorful fish and coral formations. The underwater scenery is often considered spectacular, making it an ideal spot for both beginners and experienced snorkelers.

Coron Island Hopping

Coron Island hopping offers a diverse range of experiences, from exploring enchanting lakes and lagoons to discovering underwater treasures and relaxing on idyllic beaches. The combination of natural beauty, cultural richness, and historical significance makes Coron a must-visit destination for those seeking a truly immersive island-hopping experience in Palawan. The Coron Island hopping adventure often begins with a visit to Kayangan Lake, known for its crystal-clear turquoise waters and picturesque surroundings. Visitors can climb a steep trail to a viewpoint that offers a stunning panoramic view of the lake and the surrounding limestone cliffs.

Malcapuya Island

Malcapuya Island, nestled within the Calamian Islands of Palawan, Philippines, is a paradisiacal retreat that beckons with its natural beauty and tranquility. As your boat approaches the island, you are greeted by stretches of powdery white sand that stretch along the shoreline, inviting leisurely strolls and moments of relaxation. The crystal-clear turquoise waters that surround Malcapuya Island create a mesmerizing canvas, providing a glimpse into the vibrant underwater world beneath. The island's appeal lies in its pristine beaches, where soft sands meet the gentle lapping of the sea. The tropical foliage, including swaying coconut palm trees, adds to the allure, creating a picturesque scene that embodies the essence of a tropical paradise.

Pinabuyutan Island

Nestled within the captivating archipelago of Palawan, Pinabuyutan Island stands as a testament to the natural wonders that define this tropical paradise. As your boat approaches the island's shores, you are greeted by a postcard-perfect scene—a pristine stretch of sandy beach fringed by swaying coconut palms and lush greenery. The azure waters that embrace Pinabuyutan Island create a mesmerizing contrast against the vibrant colors of the landscape. Pinabuyutan is a sanctuary for those seeking serenity and a communion with nature. The soft, powdery sand invites leisurely walks along the shoreline, offering a chance to connect with the rhythmic ebb and flow of the tide. The island's relatively secluded ambiance enhances the sense of tranquility, providing a refuge for those looking to escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life.

Pass Island

Pass Island, nestled within the stunning landscapes of Palawan, Philippines, emerges as a hidden gem in the midst of the archipelagic splendor. As your boat approaches the island's shores, the first thing that captivates is the powdery white sand that stretches along the coastline, lapped by the clear, turquoise waters of the surrounding sea. What sets Pass Island apart is its relatively untouched and serene ambiance. The island offers a retreat from the bustling world, inviting visitors to unwind on its pristine beaches and immerse themselves in the tranquility of the natural surroundings. Coconut palms sway gently in the tropical breeze, providing intermittent shade and adding to the timeless allure of the island.

Black Island

Nestled within the pristine waters of Palawan, Black Island emerges as a captivating destination, shrouded in mystery and natural beauty. As your boat approaches its shores, the first glimpse reveals a striking contrast between the lush greenery that blankets the island's interior and the dark volcanic rocks that frame its coastline, giving it the name "Black Island." Black Island beckons with its unique charm, offering a serene escape from the ordinary. The shoreline is adorned with smooth, ebony-colored rocks, creating an intriguing contrast against the azure sea. The volcanic origins of the island contribute to its distinct character, as these dark formations stand in stark juxtaposition to the vibrant hues of the surrounding tropical landscape.

Underground River Palawan

The Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park (PPSRNP) is one of the most important protected areas of the Philippines. It features a spectacular limestone or karst landscape withone of the most complex cave systems. It contains an 8.2 km long underground river that flow directly to the sea. The lower half of the river is brackish and is affected by the ocean’s tide. An underground river directly flowing into the sea, and the associated tidal influence, makes it a significant natural phenomenon. The discovery of at least 11 minerals, crystal and egg shape rock formations, and a 20 million year old Miocene age serenia fossil in the cave further add to its scientific value.

Learn More About Palawan

Palawan, often hailed as the "Last Frontier" of the Philippines, stands as an enchanting archipelago that captures the essence of untouched natural beauty and cultural richness. Nestled in the western part of the country, Palawan is renowned for its pristine landscapes, vibrant marine life, and a tapestry of indigenous cultures.

As you set foot on Palawan's shores, you are welcomed by crystal-clear turquoise waters that embrace secluded white-sand beaches, dramatic limestone cliffs, and lush emerald jungles. The province is home to some of the world's most captivating destinations, including the UNESCO World Heritage Sites of Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, where an underground river winds through awe-inspiring caverns, and the Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park, a marine sanctuary teeming with diverse marine species.

Whether you seek adventure, tranquility, or a cultural immersion, Palawan invites you to embark on a journey where every sunset paints a canvas of vivid hues, and every corner tells a story of nature's raw magnificence. Welcome to Palawan, a paradise where the rhythm of the waves and the whispers of the jungle create an unforgettable symphony of wonder.


Why.

Palawan is a destination that beckons travelers with its pristine nature, diverse marine life, cultural richness, and commitment to sustainable tourism. Whether you seek relaxation on idyllic beaches or adventure in untouched wilderness, Palawan promises an unforgettable experience.

When.

Palawan can be visited year-round, but the best time to visit is during the dry season, which typically runs from November to April. The weather during these months is characterized by clear skies and calm seas, making it ideal for beach activities, island hopping, and exploring the natural wonders of the region.

What.

Palawan is a province in the Philippines, an archipelago of more than 7,000 islands in Southeast Asia. It is known for its breathtaking natural beauty, diverse ecosystems, and vibrant marine life. The province is composed of the main island of Palawan and several smaller surrounding islands.

How.

Palawan is accessible by air and sea. The main gateway is Puerto Princesa, the capital city, which has an international airport. Other key entry points include Coron and El Nido, both known for their stunning landscapes. Once in Palawan, transportation within the province includes buses, vans, tricycles, and boats for island-hopping tours.
